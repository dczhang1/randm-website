March 15, 2025

New Grant Awarded for Environmental Decision-Making Research

We're excited to announce that the Risk and Decision Making Lab has received a $1.2 million grant from the National Science Foundation to study how communities make decisions about environmental risks and sustainability practices. This three-year project will involve collaborations with local communities, policy makers, and environmental scientists.

February 28, 2025

Dr. Johnson's Research Featured in Scientific American

Lab member Dr. Michael Johnson's groundbreaking work on the neural basis of risk perception was featured in the latest issue of Scientific American. The article highlights how our brain's reward systems influence our willingness to take financial risks and how these insights might inform financial education programs.

January 20, 2025

New Study Reveals Gender Differences in Financial Risk-Taking

Our recent publication in the Journal of Economic Psychology demonstrates significant differences in how men and women evaluate financial risks. The study, led by Dr. Sophia Chen, suggests that these differences are largely driven by socialization and experience rather than innate preferences.

December 5, 2024

Lab Members Present at International Conference on Decision Sciences

Six members of our lab presented their research at the International Conference on Decision Sciences in Singapore last week. Topics ranged from group decision-making in emergency situations to the impact of social media on risk perception. Graduate student Taylor Williams received the Best Student Presentation award.

November 10, 2024

Open Positions: Two Postdoctoral Fellowships Available

The Risk and Decision Making Lab is seeking two postdoctoral researchers to join our team in Spring 2025. One position will focus on computational modeling of decision processes, while the other will concentrate on neuroimaging approaches to studying risk perception.
