March 15, 2025

New Grant Awarded for Environmental Decision-Making Research

We're excited to announce that the Risk and Decision Making Lab has received a $1.2 million grant from the National Science Foundation to study how communities make decisions about environmental risks and sustainability practices.

This three-year project will involve collaborations with local communities, policy makers, and environmental scientists to understand how group-level decisions are made regarding climate adaptation strategies.

The project will be led by Dr. Jane Smith in collaboration with Dr. Michael Johnson, and will include opportunities for graduate and undergraduate students to participate in community-based research.

"We're thrilled to have the support of the NSF for this critical research," said Dr. Smith. "As communities face increasing environmental challenges, understanding the decision-making processes that lead to effective adaptation strategies is more important than ever."

The research will begin in June 2025 with community workshops and preliminary data collection.

February 28, 2025

Dr. Johnson's Research Featured in Scientific American

Lab member Dr. Michael Johnson's groundbreaking work on the neural basis of risk perception was featured in the latest issue of Scientific American.

The article, titled "How Your Brain Calculates Risk," highlights Dr. Johnson's recent fMRI studies that identify specific neural circuits involved in processing financial risk information.

The feature explains how these neural mechanisms influence our willingness to take financial risks and how this research might inform financial education programs. Dr. Johnson's work suggests that specific interventions could help individuals make more consistent risk assessments across different contexts.

Scientific American highlighted the practical applications of this research: "Understanding the neuroscience behind financial decision-making could lead to better tools to help people avoid common pitfalls in investment and retirement planning."

The full article is available in the March 2025 issue of Scientific American or online for subscribers.

January 20, 2025

New Study Reveals Gender Differences in Financial Risk-Taking

Our recent publication in the Journal of Economic Psychology demonstrates significant differences in how men and women evaluate financial risks.

The study, led by Dr. Sophia Chen, analyzed data from over 3,000 participants across multiple experimental conditions to identify patterns in risk assessment and decision-making.

Key findings suggest that differences in financial risk-taking are largely driven by socialization and experience rather than innate preferences. When controlling for financial knowledge and prior investment experience, gender differences were substantially reduced.

"These results challenge common assumptions about gender and risk tolerance," said Dr. Chen. "They suggest that providing equal access to financial education and experience could help close gender gaps in investment outcomes."

The paper, "Socialization vs. Predisposition: Examining Gender Differences in Financial Risk Assessment," is available through open access at the Journal of Economic Psychology.

December 5, 2024

Lab Members Present at International Conference on Decision Sciences

Six members of our lab presented their research at the International Conference on Decision Sciences in Singapore last week.

Our team shared findings on a wide range of topics, including:

  • Group decision-making in emergency situations (Alex Rodriguez)
  • The impact of social media on risk perception (Dr. Michael Johnson)
  • Neural predictors of financial risk-taking (Dr. Jane Smith)
  • Cross-cultural differences in ambiguity aversion (Dr. Sophia Chen)
  • Framing effects in healthcare decisions (Taylor Williams)
  • Computational models of decision dynamics (Sam Parker)

Graduate student Taylor Williams received the Best Student Presentation award for her work on framing effects in healthcare decisions, which combined behavioral experiments with policy analysis.

The conference provided valuable opportunities for international collaboration, with new partnerships formed with research teams from Singapore, Germany, and Australia.

November 10, 2024

Open Positions: Two Postdoctoral Fellowships Available

The Risk and Decision Making Lab is seeking two postdoctoral researchers to join our team in Spring 2025.

We are recruiting for two distinct positions:

  1. Computational Modeling Postdoc: This position will focus on developing and testing computational models of decision processes, with an emphasis on Bayesian approaches to understanding how prior beliefs influence risk perception. The ideal candidate will have strong programming skills and experience with computational modeling of cognitive processes.
  2. Neuroimaging Postdoc: This position will concentrate on using fMRI and other neuroimaging techniques to study the neural underpinnings of risk perception and decision-making under uncertainty. Experience with fMRI data collection and analysis is required.

Both positions offer competitive salaries, benefits, and research support. Postdocs will have opportunities to develop independent research lines while collaborating with our interdisciplinary team.

Application deadline: January 15, 2025. Start date: April-June 2025 (flexible).
