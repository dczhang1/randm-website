Get Involved

We offer various opportunities to engage with our research, from participating in studies to joining our lab. Explore how you can contribute to advancing our understanding of decision-making.

Participate in Studies

Volunteer as a research participant and contribute directly to our scientific investigations. Our studies involve a range of activities, from completing online surveys to participating in laboratory experiments and neuroimaging studies.

Current studies include:

  • Financial decision-making under time pressure (online, 30 minutes)
  • Group decision-making in simulated emergency scenarios (in-lab, 2 hours)
  • Brain activation during risk assessment tasks (fMRI study, 90 minutes)
  • Risk perception across different domains (online, 45 minutes)

Compensation varies by study ($15-$50). Most studies are open to adults aged 18-65.

Student Opportunities

Undergraduate Research Assistants

Gain valuable research experience by joining our lab as an undergraduate research assistant. RAs help with participant recruitment, data collection, literature reviews, and may assist with data analysis. We typically ask for a commitment of 8-10 hours per week for at least two semesters.

Graduate Programs

We accept graduate students through the Psychology, Neuroscience, and Economics departments. Prospective students should contact Dr. Jane Smith directly before applying to discuss potential fit with ongoing research.

Summer Internships

We offer 8-week summer research internships for undergraduates interested in decision science. Applications open each January for the following summer.

Professional Opportunities

Postdoctoral Fellowships

We are currently seeking two postdoctoral researchers (see details in our News section). Postdocs in our lab receive mentorship in grant writing, publishing, and career development, and have opportunities to develop independent research lines.

Research Staff

We occasionally have openings for research coordinators, lab managers, and research technicians. These positions typically require a bachelor's degree and relevant experience.

Visiting Scholars

We welcome visiting faculty and researchers interested in collaborating on projects related to risk and decision-making. Visits typically range from 2 weeks to a full semester.

Community Collaborations

We are committed to conducting research that addresses real-world challenges. We partner with:

  • Community organizations seeking to understand and improve decision-making processes
  • Policymakers interested in applying decision science insights to policy development
  • Businesses looking to enhance risk management and decision-making practices
  • Educational institutions wanting to implement evidence-based approaches to teaching decision-making skills

If you represent an organization interested in potential collaborations, please reach out to discuss how we might work together.
