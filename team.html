Meet the interdisciplinary researchers driving our work in decision science and risk analysis.

Don C. Zhang, PhD

Principal Investigator

Dr. Don Zhang is an Associate Professor of Psychology at Louisiana State University. He founded the Risk and Decision Making Lab in 2016 when he joined as faculty at Louisiana State University after completing his PhD at Bowling Green State University.

His research focuses on how people make decisions involving risks and uncertainty at work. He has received over $2.8 million in NSF Grants and is the recipient of the NSF CAREER Award. His work has been featured on news outlets such as The Guardian, New York Magazine, and The Wall Street Journal.

At LSU, Dr. Zhang serves as the Undergraduate Director as well as the Program Director for the I-O Psychology PhD program. In addition, he serves as an Associate Editor for Applied Psychology: An International Review and is the incoming chair of the Scientific Affairs Committee at the Society for Industrial and Organizational Psychology

Agnes serves as the official mascot of the Risk and Decision Making Lab. She brings joy to the lab environment and offers emotional support during high-stress research periods. Agnes reminds us to take breaks and maintain perspective during intense problem-solving sessions.
