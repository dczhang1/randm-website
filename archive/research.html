Our Research

We conduct innovative research on how people make decisions under uncertainty in workplace contexts, examining the cognitive, personality, and organizational influences on judgment and choice.

Our lab takes a rigorous, evidence-based approach to understanding decision-making phenomena. We combine experimental methods, field studies, and meta-analytic techniques to build cumulative knowledge about risk and decision making. Our research has practical implications for organizations and individuals, bridging the gap between science and practice.

Risk Taking Research

We study how individuals perceive and respond to risk in organizational contexts. Our research examines personality traits, cognitive processes, and situational factors that influence risk-taking behavior.

  • Individual differences in risk perception and tolerance
  • Workplace risk behaviors and consequences
  • Contextual factors influencing risk decisions

Employee Selection

Developing evidence-based tools and methods for identifying and selecting top talent in organizations. We investigate cognitive biases in hiring and methods to improve selection outcomes.

  • Decision-making in recruitment processes
  • Improving interview and assessment methods
  • Communicating evidence to practitioners

Decision Making Under Uncertainty

Examining how people make choices when outcomes are uncertain, including ambiguity tolerance and information processing.

Judgment and Decision Making

Investigating cognitive biases, heuristics, and systematic errors in workplace decision making and judgment.

Organizational Behavior

Understanding how organizational contexts shape individual and team decisions, including culture and climate effects.

Research Tools & Scales

We develop and validate psychological scales to measure risk-related constructs. To request access for research purposes, please contact us.

General Risk Propensity Scale (GRiPS)

An 8-item domain-general risk propensity measure aimed at capturing individual differences in risk taking across situations. Validated across multiple samples and contexts, GRiPS provides a reliable assessment of general risk-taking tendencies.

View Scale Details

Calculated and Spontaneous Risk Taking Scale (CASPRT)

A 12-item measure of risk-taking styles. This scale measures domain-general preferences for risk taking based on calculation versus spontaneity, capturing two distinct approaches to engaging with risk.
