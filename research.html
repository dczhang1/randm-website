Our Research

The Risk and Decision Making Lab conducts innovative research across several interconnected areas, using diverse methodologies from behavioral experiments to neuroimaging.

Risk Perception

We investigate how people perceive and evaluate risks across different domains, including health, finance, and environmental hazards. Our studies examine factors that influence risk assessment, such as emotion, prior experience, and cultural background.

Featured Project: Cross-Cultural Risk Perception

A multinational study comparing risk attitudes across six countries, revealing how cultural factors shape responses to uncertainty.

Decision-Making Under Uncertainty

Our research explores how individuals and groups make choices when outcomes are uncertain. We examine the cognitive processes, heuristics, and biases that influence decisions in complex environments.

Featured Project: Ambiguity vs. Risk

Experimental studies distinguishing how people respond to known probabilities (risk) versus unknown probabilities (ambiguity) in financial decisions.

Group Decision Dynamics

We study how groups navigate complex decisions, focusing on information sharing, leadership effects, and social influence in collective choice processes.

Featured Project: Emergency Response Teams

Field research examining decision-making in emergency response teams during simulated crisis scenarios.

Neural Basis of Decision-Making

Using neuroimaging techniques, we investigate the brain mechanisms underlying different aspects of decision-making, from value computation to risk assessment.

Featured Project: Neural Predictors of Risk-Taking

fMRI study identifying neural markers that predict individual differences in financial risk-taking behavior.
